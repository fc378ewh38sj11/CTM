What is this website?

This site is an archive of every known title that supports the usage of Nintendo's Mii characters across all platforms. You can sort software by how Miis are used in gameplay.

What counts as a Mii supported title?

A Mii supported title is any software that uses Nintendo's proprietary system to render Mii characters, internally known as the Face Library. Therefore, the following usages are not counted:
  • Software that uses pre-rendered Mii sprites/models (Nintendo Switch titles utilizing profile icons, Trauma Team, WarioWare: Move It!, the DS version of Final Fantasy Crystal Chronicles: Echoes of Time, etc.)
  • Fanmade titles or mods using recreations or recompilations of the Face Library
  • Cancelled titles or any software that contain Face Library data in its files, but goes unused

What do the categories mean?

Playable / Interactive

Mii characters can be selected and directly controlled or interacted with by the player. Some titles require them to be unlocked, or only usable in certain modes.

Background / Non-Playable

Mii characters stored on your system, friends list, or just randomly-generated ones may appear in the background of gameplay, or have a role in a title where they cannot be directly controlled. These roles include bystanders, spectators, inhabitants of a title's setting, and more.

Icon / Avatar

Mii characters will represent a player or character in gameplay, save files, StreetPass and SpotPass encounters, Miiverse communication, or other UI elements. Sometimes they're static, expressive, 2D, 3D... it all depends on the title. This role is usually pretty boring, but some software may use Mii portraits in pretty neat ways!

Why though?

Miis are incredibly verstaile characters and can be considered some of the most adaptable characters in gaming history. While many know them just for their appearances in casual motion controlled games or quirkier smaller titles such as the Wii and Tomodachi Life series, their roles in all sorts of games and genres are often very overlooked.

They've met all sorts of characters and real-life public figures including Mario, Sonic, Mickey Mouse, Hatsune Miku, Alex Trebek, Howie Mandel, Billy Blanks, Danica Patrick, Lionel Messi, and so many more. They've been Olympic medalists, heroes, dancers, racers, doctors, army recruits... your Mii is the everyman who has done it all, and it makes archiving each and every one of their appearances fun.
