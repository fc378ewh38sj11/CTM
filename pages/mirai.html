Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai

Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai is a rhythm video game released for the Nintendo 3DS only in Japan on March 8, 2012. The game is a spinoff of the Hatsune Miku: Project DIVA series.

Mii usage

Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai allows players to use their personal Mii character in the game's AR mode. Using AR cards similar to the ones made for AR Games, the player can place their Mii in an AR scene and can change their position and size to their liking alongside Vocaloid characters. Once unlocking all outfits in the game, the ability to watch your Mii perform certain PVs (promotional videos) from the game through the AR mode will become available. Players can take pictures of these scenes and save them onto their system or a SD card.

Trivia

  • The game's default outfit for Miis (a white t-shirt with the word 'mirai' printed on it with jean shorts) is left over in the game's sequel Hatsune Miku: Project Mirai 2 and its enhanced version Project Mirai DX.
    • The sequel's official support page even leaves a disclaimer that the previous entry's Mii feature will not return.
  • By modifying the game's debug menu via hacking, it is possible to use a Mii character in every PV the game includes, albeit with few graphical errors as a result of the Mii head.
