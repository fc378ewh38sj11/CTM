Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai

Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai is a rhythm video game released for the Nintendo 3DS only in Japan on March 8, 2012. The game is a spinoff of the Hatsune Miku: Project DIVA series.

Mii usage

Hatsune Miku and Future Stars: Project Mirai allows players to use their personal Mii character in the game's AR mode. Using AR cards similar to the ones made for AR Games, the player can scan in these cards to watch certain PVs (promotional videos) of songs featured in the game. The mode has a toggle to include a Mii alongside a Vocaloid character where they can be resized and positioned, and players can take pictures of these scenes to store on their system.

Trivia

  • Despite not being featured in the game's main modes, the debug menu considers the player's Mii a standard character, allowing the Mii to cycle between expressions.
    • With this in mind, it is possible to replace a character's head with a Mii in debug testing and use them in all non-AR mode PVs via modding.
  • The game's default outfit for Miis (a white shirt with the word 'mirai' printed on it with jean shorts) is left over in the game's sequel, Hatsune Miku: Project Mirai 2.
    • The sequel's official website even leaves a disclaimer that the previous entry's Mii feature was not brought back.
