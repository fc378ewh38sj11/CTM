Pop-Up Pirate

Pop-Up Pirate (localized as Party Fun Pirate in the US) is a video game adaption of the luck-based children's toy of the same name developed by Takara Tomy. It was first released for the WiiWare service on November 25, 2008.

Mii usage

A Mii character can be selected to replace Pirate Pete after the player completes a few matches of Party Game Pirate and Pop-Up Logic; the game's two main modes. Miis in this title are voiced, but use sped up versions of Pirate Pete's voice clips regardless of the Mii's gender.

Oddities

  • Consistently switching Miis in the character menu will cause the game to render a headless Mii in the barrel. This goes away after leaving the menu and does not affect gameplay.

  • Mii characters' body model does not change skin tones.
