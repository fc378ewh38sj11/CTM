Pop'n Music

Pop'n Music (known as Pop'n Rhythm in Europe) is a rhythm video game first released for the Wii on August 6th, 2009. It was developed by Konami and is a reboot of the Pop'n Music rhythm game series; being one of the few titles in the series to launch outside of Asia.

Mii usage

Mii characters appear as playable and background characters in Pop'n Music. To play as a Mii, the player must fully clear a number of the game's songs. Since the game has a flat 2D art style, Miis are rendered as 2D icons placed onto several body sprites which differ based on stage selection. They are also sorted by alphabetical order on the character select screen.

Despite being flat 2D icons, Miis are expressive in this title. A Mii will cycle between their angry and sad expressions while sweating when failing a song, and will look happy while performing a song well.

Oddities

  • Despite the game being rated for all ages in every region, this is one of the few Mii supported games to include explicit language: The Fear by Lily Allen appears as a selectable song, and is completely uncensored.
